Overview

At Muhlenberg College, I studied Computer Science while also exploring entrepreneurship, writing, art, campus leadership, event planning, and community-building. A lot of my college experience has centered around creating things: clubs, websites, events, communities, written pieces, and projects that connect different parts of my interests.

Academics

Computer Science major, with interests spanning web development, entrepreneurship, writing, design, and creative technology.

Leadership

Creative Work

My college experience also connected with writing, visual art, websites, social media, storytelling, and larger projects outside of class.

Clubs & Leadership

One of the biggest parts of my time at Muhlenberg has been building, reviving, organizing, and helping run student communities.

Pokémon Club

Founder and President. A casual campus community for Pokémon fans, including games, trivia, trading card game events, retro consoles, discussion, and relaxed hangouts.

Fencing Club

President. Helped revive and organize the club, coordinate practices, communicate with instructors, and build a space for students interested in fencing.

MBGA

President of the Muhlenberg Board Game Association. Helped continue and organize weekly board game meetings and social gaming events.

Harry Potter Club

Founder and President. A discussion-based club focused on Harry Potter, lore, characters, themes, theories, and the world of the series.

MAC

Member of the Muhlenberg Activities Council, helping with campus programming, late-night events, trivia, themed events, and student activities.

Other Involvement

Additional campus involvement included organizations, events, performances, social groups, and one-off projects throughout my time at Muhlenberg.

Campus Experiences

Beyond classes and clubs, Muhlenberg gave me opportunities to work on communications, student leadership, event planning, public-facing writing, and professional development.

  • Club Leadership: organized meetings, planned events, coordinated rooms and schedules, communicated with members, and helped maintain active campus communities.
  • Event Planning: helped plan and support campus events through clubs and MAC, including trivia, games, themed events, and community activities.
